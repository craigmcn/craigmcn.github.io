This week's instalment of Music Monday is [*The Man Who Sold the World*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Man_Who_Sold_the_World_(song)) by [David Bowie](https://www.davidbowie.com/) from their 1970 album, [The Man Who Sold the World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Man_Who_Sold_the_World_(album)). The [Nirvana](https://www.nirvana.com/) version is from their 1994 album, [MTV Unplugged in New York](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MTV_Unplugged_in_New_York).

Nirvana's Kurt Cobain was introduced to the song by drummer Chad Channing, who had bought a used copy of the album, and was surprised to discover it was by Bowie. Their 1993 MTV Unplugged performance brought the song to a new generation of listeners, and Bowie later re-recorded an acoustic version, which he described as a nod to Nirvana popularising it.
